## Why Mississauga Accounting Firms Need Specialist IT Support

Accounting and financial advisory practices handle some of the most sensitive data that exists: client tax returns, financial statements, investment portfolios, and business valuations. This data is valuable to cybercriminals and subject to strict regulatory obligations under PIPEDA and CPA Canada's professional standards.

A security incident at an accounting firm does not just create financial and regulatory exposure — it destroys the client trust that the practice is built on. Clients who have shared their complete financial picture with you need absolute confidence that information is protected.

### The Threat Landscape for Financial Professionals

Accounting firms face two primary cyber threats that are distinct from most other industries.

**Business email compromise (BEC)** is the most financially damaging. Fraudsters compromise or impersonate an accountant's email account and use it to redirect client payments, request fraudulent wire transfers during real estate transactions, or intercept tax refunds. The financial losses from BEC attacks can be immediate and difficult to recover.

**Tax-related fraud** involves the theft of client information — Social Insurance Numbers, income data, and tax history — which fraudsters use to file fraudulent tax returns in clients' names and collect refunds before the legitimate return is filed. Protecting this data is both an ethical obligation and a regulatory requirement.
