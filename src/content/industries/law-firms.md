## Why Mississauga Law Firms Need a Specialist MSP

A law firm is not like other small businesses. The data you hold — client communications, case files, financial records, opposing counsel strategies — is among the most sensitive information that any organisation handles. The professional consequences of a data breach extend beyond financial loss to regulatory scrutiny, reputational damage, and potential discipline by the Law Society of Ontario.

Most generalist IT companies do not understand these stakes. WiseTech works with legal practices in Mississauga and the GTA and understands the specific obligations and risks that lawyers and paralegals face.

### The Legal Profession's IT Security Obligations

The Law Society of Ontario's Rules of Professional Conduct require lawyers to maintain the confidentiality of client information and to take reasonable steps to protect it from unauthorised disclosure. In practice, this means implementing appropriate technical safeguards — encrypted email communications, access-controlled file storage, documented security policies, and a breach response plan.

These are not optional recommendations. A firm that suffers a preventable data breach and cannot demonstrate it took reasonable precautions faces potential discipline, civil liability, and significant reputational harm.

### Why Law Firms Are Targeted

Cybercriminals target law firms deliberately. A successful ransomware attack on a legal practice creates enormous pressure to pay: client confidentiality obligations, court deadlines, and the sensitivity of the data at stake all make law firms more likely to pay a ransom than a typical small business.

Business email compromise (BEC) is another significant risk — fraudsters who compromise a lawyer's email account can intercept real estate transactions, redirect trust account funds, or steal client information. WiseTech's email security solutions specifically address these attack vectors.
