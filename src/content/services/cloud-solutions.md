## Why Mississauga Businesses Are Moving to the Cloud

The shift to cloud-based productivity tools is no longer a question of if — it is a question of when and how. Businesses still running on-premise email servers, local file shares, or legacy software are paying more in maintenance, experiencing more downtime, and exposing themselves to security risks that cloud platforms largely eliminate.

Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace give your team the ability to work from anywhere, collaborate in real time, and access enterprise-grade security and compliance features that would cost hundreds of thousands of dollars to replicate on-premise.

### What Cloud Migration Actually Involves

A cloud migration is more than dragging files to a new folder. A proper migration covers email history and contacts, calendar data, shared files and folder structures, application integrations, security and permission configurations, and user training.

WiseTech manages every stage of this process. We start with a pre-migration audit of your current environment, build a migration plan specific to your business, execute the migration with zero data loss, and then ensure your team is comfortable and productive on the new platform.

### Microsoft 365 vs Google Workspace

Both platforms are excellent — the right choice depends on your team's existing workflows and technical requirements. Microsoft 365 is typically the right fit for businesses deeply embedded in the Microsoft ecosystem, those with advanced compliance requirements, or industries that rely heavily on Excel and Word. Google Workspace suits teams that prefer simplicity, browser-first workflows, and strong real-time collaboration.

WiseTech is experienced with both platforms and will guide you to the right decision based on your specific situation — not on which platform pays better margins.

### Ongoing Cloud Management

The migration is just the beginning. WiseTech's managed IT plans include ongoing administration of your cloud platform — adding and removing users, managing licences, configuring security policies, troubleshooting issues, and keeping your subscription right-sized as your team grows.
